Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that combines a
retrieval system with a language model. Instead of relying only on the
model's parametric memory, RAG retrieves relevant documents from an
external corpus and conditions the generated answer on that retrieved
context. This grounds answers in real evidence and reduces hallucination.
A typical RAG pipeline embeds a corpus into vectors, stores them in a
vector index, retrieves the top-k most similar chunks for a query, and
passes them to the language model as context.
